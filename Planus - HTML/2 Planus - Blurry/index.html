Who Am I

The young programmer.

I began programming at age 16 and have been in love ever since. My first experience with programming was an HTML class offered by my high school. I finished the course curriculum 6 weeks early and spent those last 6 weeks building everything I could imagine.

Before graduating high school, I completed the remaining two programming courses offered by my school, a course on visual basic and another called: Intro to Java. Programming in school was never enough for me, I began to practice my skills from home. After high school, I attended the University of Wisconsin Oshkosh to pursue my Bachelors Degree in Software Engineering.

Where I am today

I am now 22 years old and programming has become an even bigger part of my life. I am constantly learning the latest technologies, building web and mobile apps, and getting involved with open source projects. I am currently a TA at Coding House, a coding bootcamp in the San Fransisco Bay Area. I am here because being a developer is the most important thing in my life.

Life outside of code.

When I am not programming I enjoy spending time with my family and two dogs. I also enjoy hanging out with my friends and getting involved in pickup soccer games whenever possible.
